Senior Project

Drowsiness Detection

Each year, drowsy driving accounts for about 100,000 crashes, 71,000 injuries and 1,550 fatalities, according to the National Safety Council (NSC). The real number may be much higher, however, as it is difficult to determine whether a driver was drowsy at the time of a crash.

Because of this serious problem, in order to make the driver aware before any such accident occurs, I have developed this program. It detects if a live human has had their eyes closed for more than a second. This sort of technology is useful for anybody interested in increased driving safety, including commercial & everyday drivers, car companies, and vehicle-insurance companies. You check out the source code in here!

I used and processed more than 3000 images from MRL eye dataset to train my drowsiness detection model.
